## Core Principles

### 1. Be Authentic
Share genuine experiences, not marketing copy. Use your own voice.

### 2. Provide Value
Every post should educate, inspire, or solve a problem for readers.

### 3. Be Transparent
Disclose your involvement with the project. Honesty builds trust.

### 4. Respect Privacy
Never share confidential information, unreleased features, or sensitive data.

### 5. Stay Professional
Maintain a positive, constructive tone. Avoid controversial topics.

---

## Disclosure Requirements

### When to Disclose
Always disclose your role when:
- Posting about dbt-Workbench features
- Sharing project updates or achievements
- Asking for feedback or contributions
- Promoting the project in any way

### How to Disclose
Use one of these approaches:

**Option 1: In the post**
```
"I'm a contributor to dbt-Workbench, an open-source UI for dbt..."
```

**Option 2: In your LinkedIn profile**
- Add "Contributor at dbt-Workbench" or "Maintainer of dbt-Workbench" to your profile
- Link to the GitHub repository

**Option 3: In comments**
If you forget to disclose in the post, clarify in the comments immediately.

## Best Practices

### Timing
**Best times to post:**
- Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday: 8-10 AM or 5-6 PM (your audience's timezone)
- Friday: Lighter, more casual content works well
- Weekends: Lower engagement, but longer-form content can perform well

**Avoid:**
- Holidays (unless tech/developer-focused)
- Major industry conferences (unless you're posting from there)

### Frequency
- **1-2 posts per week** is ideal
- Quality over quantity - don't spam
- Space out posts about the same topic by at least 1 week

### Engagement
- **Respond to all comments** within 24 hours
- **Ask follow-up questions** to keep conversations going
- **Tag team members** when relevant (if they'd want to be tagged)
- **Share other people's content** occasionally (builds goodwill)

### Visuals
- **Use real screenshots** of dbt-Workbench UI
- **Add GIFs** for demos (Loom, ScreenToGif)
- **Create diagrams** for technical concepts (Figma, Draw.io)
- **Stock photos sparingly** - prefer real images

### Hashtags
**Use 3-5 relevant hashtags:**
```
#dbt #DataEngineering #OpenSource #AnalyticsEngineering #SQL
```
**Mix broad and specific:**
- Broad: `#dbt #DataEngineering #OpenSource`
- Specific: `#DataLineage #SQLWorkspace #AirGappedDeployment`

## What to Avoid

### ‚ùå Don't
- Copy-paste marketing copy directly from company posts
- Overuse hashtags (more than 5-7 is spammy)
- Post without disclosing your involvement
- Make claims you can't back up
- Ignore negative comments (respond professionally)
- Post competitor comparisons (keep it positive)
- Share confidential or unreleased information
- Post when frustrated or emotional (take 24h to cool off)

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Be Careful With
- Technical jargon (explain concepts for broader audience)
- Humor (what's funny to you might not land)
- Controversial topics outside of tech (avoid)
- Over-promoting (mix in other content too)
